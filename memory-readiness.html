Tells you which of the five scars your AI memory stack is currently paying for — and how much architectural debt is compounding while you wait.

How this works. Six yes/no questions. Each one corresponds to a memory failure mode we have seen kill AI deployments across 50+ Fortune-500 engagements. Each "yes" = one architectural debt your stack is paying interest on. Score 0/6 means you're a memory architect already; score 5–6 means memory failures are now business risk. Output is yours; nothing leaves your browser.

  • Have you ever asked your AI assistant a question in a non-English language and gotten a noticeably worse answer than the English equivalent? Multilingual gap
  • Has your AI assistant ever failed to recall an exact term, ID, or proper noun you know is in prior conversation? Single-store retrieval trap
  • Are you running an LLM call on every inbound user message — or planning to, as your assistant scales? Per-message extraction cost
  • Did someone on your team hand-design the folder/category/tag structure the AI uses to organize its memories? Hand-crafted hierarchy
  • When you ask your AI assistant about something from 6+ months ago, do stale or superseded answers compete with the current ones? No decay layer
  • If your model vendor changed pricing, exited your jurisdiction, or had its data-transfer adequacy revoked tomorrow, would your team's accumulated memory be at risk? Architecture · Sovereignty gap
