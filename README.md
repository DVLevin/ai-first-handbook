## Why this exists

Most companies are *adding* AI: a chatbot here, a copilot there, a few prompts in Slack. The dashboards look busy. The compounding never arrives.

A few companies are doing something fundamentally different — **re-architecting their operating model so AI is the substrate, not a feature**. They ship faster, retain knowledge across teams, and watch every project make the next one cheaper. The gap between "added AI" and "AI-first" is now measured in quarters, and it's widening every month.

This handbook is the field guide for getting on the right side of that gap.

If your team is "doing AI" but not seeing compounding returns, **this is the playbook to fix it**.

## What's inside

This repo publishes two formats of the same content. Pick whichever your audience needs:

- **[The Handbook](https://dvlevin.github.io/ai-first-handbook/handbook.html)** — the long-read essay (~30 min). Single page, printable, designed to be sent to a CEO who will read it on a flight.
- **[The Deck](https://dvlevin.github.io/ai-first-handbook/deck.html)** — 37 slides (~25 min talk). Built for live talks, founder offsites, board calls, all-hands. Press `F` for fullscreen, `?` for overview, arrow keys to navigate.

Both formats walk the same arc:

1. **The thesis** — why "added AI" plateaus and "AI-first" compounds
2. **The four shifts** — SaaS→vault, hand-authored→AI-drafted, files→graph, heroics→memory
3. **The architecture** — Vault · Schema · Agents · Graph (each cheap to install, expensive to skip)
4. **The init-* family** — five commands that turn a folder into a company OS in ~90 minutes
5. **The AI-PM pipeline** — Conversa → Prody → PM-Vault, with a pre-commit hook as the safety net
6. **This week** — day-by-day, what to install, what to read, when to co-work
7. **Culture** — the four primitives (trees, PRs, merges, journal) that make it stick
8. **The first 90 days** — milestones to expect, week 1 through month 3
9. **Failure modes** — the six anti-patterns that kill this if you're not paying attention
